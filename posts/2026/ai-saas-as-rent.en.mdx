Just checked the bill: paid xAI **$300/mo** for Grok Heavy.

![Fig 1 — xAI Grok Heavy payment screenshot](/posts/ai-saas-as-rent/fig-1-xai-grok-heavy-payment.webp)

*Fig 1 — xAI / Grok Heavy payment.*

As someone already paying Claude Code MAX (20x, **$200/mo**): this is painful. I didn’t expect to keep spending more and more on this.

As the saying goes — Chamath once said that as a successful man today, the only thing you have to do is **think less**, then keep walking forward with your eyes closed.

[Chamath on X](https://x.com/chamath)

Why painful? Because the price keeps climbing, and running both tools at once already feels like basic kit — if you want to survive in this post-AI era.

And hey, you probably say, why don't you host your own AI agent and so on?

That sounds a lot like: *Why don't you just buy a house in Taipei or Hong Kong rather than complaining the rent here?*

Sounds weird. A little uncanny.

It feels like a problem you can’t exit. If we treat these monthly AI SaaS fees as “rent” for residents of the new world — is that fair? And how do you show that comparison and the research behind it?

Of course, the psychological damage of 30+ white-collar workers being wiped out by AI is another essay. That SF software eng salaries are too high, and AI is flattening East Asian developer pay toward SF — also another essay.
